Kindness App Persona and Use Case

Persona Profile

Name: Sarah Goodheart

Technology:
- iPhone 14 with iOS 17 using Safari browser
- Dell XPS laptop running Windows 11 with Chrome browser
- Active social media user on Instagram and Facebook

Love/Hate:
- Loves documenting positive moments and sharing uplifting content
- Enjoys exploring local community events and activities
- Dislikes negativity on social media and seeks positive spaces
- Gets frustrated with complicated app interfaces

Proficiency:
- Intermediate technology user
- Comfortable with basic social media features
- Uses map applications daily for navigation
- Regular mobile app user for social networking

Attitudes and Behaviors:
- Actively looks for ways to spread positivity in her community
- Frequently volunteers at local events
- Takes daily walks in her neighborhood
- Values authentic human connections
- Enjoys sharing inspiring stories with friends and family
- Wants to encourage others to perform acts of kindness
- Prefers visual content with location context
- Regularly checks in at local businesses and community spots

User Story

As a community-minded user, I want to share acts of kindness I witness in my neighborhood to inspire others and track the spread of positivity in my area.

Use Case

Sarah witnessed someone helping an elderly person carry groceries at her local supermarket and wants to share this act of kindness on the app.

Pre-condition: Sarah is logged into her account and on the home screen.
Post-condition: The act of kindness is posted and visible on both the feed and map.

Interaction Flow

  1. Sarah taps the "Add Kindness" button on the home screen
  2. App opens the new post creation screen
  3. Sarah types in description of the grocery-helping incident
  4. App presents location tagging options
  5. Sarah selects "Current Location" or searches for the supermarket
  6. App displays map for location confirmation
  7. Sarah confirms location
  8. App prompts for optional photo upload
  9. Sarah skips photo upload
  10. Sarah taps "Post" button
  11. App processes the post and adds it to both feed and map
  12. App displays confirmation with a heart animation on the map
  13. Sarah views her post on the main feed with the location marker
